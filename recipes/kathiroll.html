Kathi Roll

A Kathiroll roll is a popular street food from India, especially associated with Kolkata. It's a type of wrap where paratha (or sometimes other flatbreads) is filled with various ingredients like marinated meat (chicken, mutton, or beef), paneer (for a vegetarian version), onions, sauces, and sometimes an egg, all rolled up together.

The filling is often spiced with tangy sauces, and it’s typically served hot with a side of chutney or a salad. The beauty of a kathi roll lies in its versatility; you can customize it with different fillings, sauces, and veggies based on your taste preferences. They make for a delicious, portable snack or meal.

Imagine biting into a warm, crispy paratha, slightly charred from the griddle, wrapped around a succulent, spiced filling that dances with flavor. The tender pieces of marinated chicken (or juicy paneer, if you're vegetarian) are kissed with smoky, tangy spices, each bite bursting with zest. A layer of caramelized onions adds just the right touch of sweetness, while a drizzle of cool, creamy yogurt or spicy chutney balances the heat. The wrap is soft yet crispy, a perfect contrast of textures that makes every mouthful irresistible. It's a flavor-packed, mouthwatering masterpiece that leaves you craving more with every bite.

Ingredients

For the Marinated Chicken:

  • 500g boneless chicken (cut into strips or cubes)
  • 2 tablespoons yogurt
  • 1 tablespoon ginger-garlic paste
  • 1 tablespoon red chili powder
  • 1 teaspoon garam masala
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1 tablespoon lemon juice
  • Salt to taste
  • 1 tablespoon oil (for marination)

For the Paratha (Flatbread):

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1 tablespoon oil or ghee
  • Water (as needed to make the dough)
  • A pinch of sugar (optional, to help with browning)

For the Assembly:

  • 1 large onion, thinly sliced
  • 1 cucumber, thinly sliced (optional)
  • Fresh coriander leaves (chopped)
  • 1-2 green chilies, thinly sliced (optional)
  • 1-2 tablespoons mayonnaise or yogurt (optional for creaminess)
  • 1-2 tablespoons tangy tamarind chutney or mint chutney
  • A squeeze of lemon juice
  • 1 egg (optional, if you'd like an egg wrap)

Instructions of cooking

  1. Marinate the chicken:

    2. Combine all the marinade ingredients, mix well, and let the chicken sit in the fridge for at least 30 minutes to soak up the flavors.

  2. Make the parathas:

    3. Mix flour, salt, oil, and water to form a soft dough. Divide the dough into balls and roll them out into thin circles. Cook on a hot griddle or tawa until golden and crispy.

  3. Cook the chicken:

    4. Heat some oil in a pan and cook the marinated chicken until tender and slightly charred. You can also cook it on a grill for an added smoky flavor.

  4. Assemble the roll:

    5. Place the cooked chicken on a paratha, top with onions, cucumber, coriander, chilies, chutney, and a squeeze of lemon. Optionally, add an egg scrambled on the paratha before rolling.

  5. Roll it up:

    6. Gently fold the paratha over the filling to form a tight roll, and it’s ready to devour!
